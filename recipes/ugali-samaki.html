Traditional Ugali Samaki

Description

Ugali Samaki is a beloved dish in East Africa, combining a staple maize porridge (Ugali) with a savory fish stew (Samaki). This dish is not only nourishing but also rich in flavor, offering a delightful combination of textures and tastes. It's a wholesome meal that's enjoyed in many households and is perfect for both everyday dinners and special occasions.

The recipe features a simple yet flavorful preparation method, making it accessible for home cooks of all levels. Serve it hot and enjoy the authentic taste of East African cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups maize flour (cornmeal)
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 pound fish fillets (tilapia or any firm white fish)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Steps

  1. To make the ugali, bring 4 cups of water to a boil in a large pot. Gradually add the maize flour, stirring continuously to prevent lumps. Reduce the heat and continue to stir until the mixture thickens and pulls away from the sides of the pot. Cover and let it cook for a few more minutes, then set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the onions and garlic, and saut√© until the onions are translucent.
  3. Add the tomatoes and bell pepper to the skillet. Cook for about 5 minutes, or until the tomatoes are soft.
  4. Stir in the turmeric powder, curry powder, salt, and pepper. Cook for another minute to release the flavors.
  5. Add the fish fillets to the skillet, then pour in the coconut milk. Bring to a simmer and cook for about 10-15 minutes, or until the fish is cooked through and the sauce has thickened.
  6. Garnish the fish stew with fresh cilantro.
  7. Serve the fish stew hot with ugali on the side. Enjoy your traditional Ugali Samaki!
